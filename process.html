Our Property Evaluation Process

Discover how we assess every property to decide the right care strategy — from a quick observation walk to calling in the experts.

Download Property Check Guide (.docx)

Evaluation Workflow

Five clear steps from first look to final decision

1

Observe

Walk the property and document visible conditions: grass height, bare patches, weed coverage, discolouration, drainage issues, and equipment accessibility. Use our downloadable checklist to record findings systematically.

2

Assess Risk

Score each category (lawn health, pest activity, drainage, terrain difficulty, equipment suitability) on a 1–5 scale. A combined score above 17 is a strong signal that professional intervention is warranted.

3

Consider Season & Timing

Factor in the current season. Spring and early summer demand the most frequent attention; winter typically requires minimal mowing but heightened drainage and frost management. Timing heavily influences the best approach.

4

DIY vs. Expert Decision

Based on your observations and risk score, weigh the DIY option against calling a professional. Consider property size, equipment on hand, time availability, and the desired quality of outcome before deciding.

5

Recommended Actions

Act on your decision: schedule regular self-maintenance, book a one-time professional assessment, or request a full ongoing care programme. Review and repeat the evaluation cycle each season.

Decision Guide

Quick rules to help you choose the right approach

Situation DIY Call an Expert
Lawn area Under 500 m² Over 500 m² or complex layout
Equipment available Standard mower + trimmer No suitable equipment
Visible pests or disease Minor, isolated spots Widespread or recurring
Terrain Flat, unobstructed Steep slopes or many obstacles
Time available Regular weekly window Time-constrained schedule
Desired outcome Tidy and maintained Show-quality or pristine
Risk score (from checklist) 0 – 8 17 or higher

DIY vs. Professional Care

An honest comparison to help you decide

DIY Lawn Care

Pros

  • No labour costs
  • Flexible, on-demand scheduling
  • Personal satisfaction and control
  • Immediate response to issues

Cons

  • Time-intensive each week
  • Equipment purchase and upkeep costs
  • Risk of incorrect mowing height or techniques
  • Limited expertise for complex issues

Professional Lawn Care

Pros

  • Expert knowledge and proven techniques
  • Commercial-grade equipment
  • Saves significant time and effort
  • Guaranteed results with service warranty
  • Ongoing maintenance plans available

Cons

  • Higher recurring cost
  • Scheduled visits (less spontaneous)
  • Requires vetting and trusting a provider
